# Session Topic

Herbicide-Free Campus, environmental risks, and active IELTS speaking practice

## 1. Band 7.5-8.0 Model Answer

Whether harmful chemicals should be used on a university campus depends on their purpose and how strictly they are controlled. If a chemical is essential for laboratory research and safety rules are followed, its use may be acceptable. However, if it harms people's health or contaminates waterways, the university should stop using it and look for safer alternatives. Technological development can come at a cost when environmental risks are ignored, so universities, students, and researchers should all play a role in protecting the environment.

## 2. New Vocabulary

### Technical vs Technological

Meaning:
Technical relates to practical skills, specialist details, or the operation of something. Technological relates to technology and broader technological development.

Pronunciation:
technical /ˈtek.nɪ.kəl/; technological /ˌtek.nəˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl/

Example:
We had a technical problem during the experiment, but technological progress has improved the equipment.

IELTS usage:
Use technical for problems, skills, details, and support. Use technological for progress, development, change, and innovation.

Related words:
technology, technique, innovation, equipment

## 3. Grammar Upgrade

### It depends on the purpose

Original:
It depends if this kind of material is safe.

Better:
It depends on the purpose. If this kind of material is safe, it might be acceptable.

Explanation:
“Depend”通常和介词“on”搭配。把条件拆成两个句子后，逻辑更清楚，也更像自然的雅思口语表达。

### An outlet

Original:
They need to find a outlet.

Better:
They need to find an outlet.

Explanation:
“Outlet”以元音音素开头，因此冠词用“an”，不是“a”。

### Harmful to

Original:
If it is harmful for all human beings, we should stop it.

Better:
If it is harmful to people or the environment, the university should stop using it.

Explanation:
固定搭配是“be harmful to someone or something”。同时，“stop using it”比“stop it”更明确。

### Stop someone from doing something

Original:
I will stop them doing these kinds of things.

Better:
I will ask the university to stop using the chemicals, or stop people from using them irresponsibly.

Explanation:
“停止做某事”用“stop doing something”；“阻止某人做某事”用“stop someone from doing something”。

## 4. Pronunciation Notes

### Technological

## 6. Mini Speaking Responses

### Balanced view on chemical use

Response:
It depends on the purpose. If a chemical is essential for laboratory research and safety rules are followed, its use might be acceptable. However, if it harms people's health or the environment, the university should stop using it and find safer alternatives.

Used for:
Environment, Education, Health, Opinion Questions

Structure:
condition -> acceptable case -> harmful case -> recommendation

Related:
safety rules, laboratory research, safer alternatives

### Environmental responsibility

Response:
Technological development can come at a cost if environmental risks are ignored. Chemical waste can contaminate waterways, so governments, universities, companies, and ordinary people should all play a role in protecting the environment.

Used for:
Technology, Environment, Society, Opinion Questions

Structure:
warning -> concrete consequence -> shared responsibility

Related:
technological development, contaminated waterways, environmental responsibility

### Finding an outlet for pressure

Response:
When people are under pressure from their studies or research, they need to find an outlet. Talking with friends, practising English, or following a workout routine can help them relax and improve their mood.

Used for:
Mental Health, Education, Lifestyle, Study

Structure:
problem -> need -> examples -> benefit

Related:
pressure, anxiety, exercise, emotional outlet

## 7. Learning Feedback

### Strengths

- The learner could give a balanced answer instead of treating chemical use as simply good or bad.
- New collocations were actively used in original sentences, especially “come at a cost”, “contaminate waterways”, and “play a role in”.
- The learner could explain ideas at length and connect environmental issues with technology, health, and responsibility.

### Areas to Improve

- Use articles carefully: “an outlet”, not “a outlet”.
- Use natural prepositions: “harmful to”, “depend on”, and “pressure from studies”.
- Avoid repeated fillers and long, unfinished sentence chains. State the main judgment first, then explain it.
- Practise long words separately so the first syllables are not dropped, especially “technological” and “environmental”.

### Final Active Output

Technological development can come at a cost if environmental risks are ignored. Chemical waste can contaminate waterways, so we should play a role in protecting the environment.
